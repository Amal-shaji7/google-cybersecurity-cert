# Course 5 — Assets, Threats, and Vulnerabilities

## Overview

This folder contains all deliverables completed during
**Course 5: Assets, Threats, and Vulnerabilities** of
the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate.

This course covered how organisations identify and
classify assets, assess threats and vulnerabilities,
and apply structured frameworks to analyse and manage
security risk. The activities move from foundational
asset management through to hands-on threat modelling
and vulnerability analysis.

## Key Concepts Covered

### Asset Security
- Asset classification and inventory management
- Data sensitivity tiers — Restricted, Confidential,
Internal-only, Public
- The asset management lifecycle
- Information privacy and data handling practices
- Principle of least privilege applied to assets

### Threats and Vulnerabilities
- Threat categories — insider, external, APT,
supply chain
- Physical attack vectors — USB drives,
removable media
- Social engineering and human-based threats
- Vulnerability assessment methodology

### Risk Management
- Risk scoring — likelihood and severity matrices
- Calculating risk priority for remediation planning
- Communicating risk to stakeholders

### Access Controls — AAA Framework
- Authentication, Authorisation, Accounting
- Multi-factor authentication
- Separation of duties

### Threat Modelling
- PASTA (Process for Attack Simulation and Threat
Analysis) — seven stage methodology
- Connecting asset value, attacker motivation,
and vulnerability

### Cryptography Fundamentals
- Symmetric vs asymmetric encryption
- PKI and certificates
- Hashing and data integrity
